Redesigning the Calendar in the Canvas Mobile App

App Flow Redesign Case Study

By: Courtney Miyashiro

Overview

As undergraduates at James Madison University and among many other students at universities and secondary schools, the Canvas platform allows students as well as educators to stay up to date on their current courses. Whether it be keeping track of assignments, due dates, grades, announcements, and messages, Canvas is the go to platform for students to be able to manage school whenever and wherever they want. If a school such as James Madison University uses Canvas as the main platform for course management, students could heavily rely on this app and utilize it day in and day out. The user experience and ease is extremely crucial and plays a role in student success.

The Canvas mobile app features 5 main parts: Courses, Calendar, To Do, Notifications, and Inbox. During my time at JMU, I have used this app every day and have gotten to know it very well. As with any app, there are pros and cons, but there is one particular feature that needs some improvement: the Calendar. To some students, a calendar is just a calendar and some may not even use it on Canvas. Since students use the Canvas app at the very least once or twice a day, students should be able to access a well organized and visually appealing calendar on the app. Keeping track of assignments, quizzes, tests, and more importantly, their due dates, is essential for student success and that is why I wanted to redesign the Canvas Calendar in hopes of making it a studentâ€™s first choice of how they manage courses rather than an outside app or organizational platform.

Defining Users and Goals

The primary users of the Canvas mobile app are generally students who are enrolled at schools who use Canvas as the main course management system. More specifically, I decided to focus on the students at James Madison University.

The goals of the users include the following:

  • Utilizing the Calendar
  • Accessing materials for courses
  • Managing assignments, quizzes, and tests
  • Completing and submitting assignments
  • Receiving notifications for assignments, quizzes, and tests
  • Viewing grades and feedback
  • Receiving messages from professors and students
  • Sending messages to professors and students

User Research

My user research included 25 students from James Madison University each ranging from freshman to senior and from the ages 18 to 22 years old. The survey was anonymous and answers to survey questions were collected using a Google Form. 

Out of the 25 students who responded, 52% or 13 students specifically stated that they use the Canvas mobile app daily. 40% or 10 students stated that they use the app at least 4-6 times a week.

Out of the 25 students who responded, 56% or 14 students stated that they do use the Calendar on Canvas.

In regards to students’ experiences with the Calendar on the app, 56% or 14 out of 25 students stated that they’ve had an average experience. Additionally, 28% or 7 students stated that they’ve had a poor experience with the Calendar.

Additionally, 68% or 17 out of the 25 students who responded to the survey expressed that they do find it difficult to keep track of due dates as well as assignments/tests/quizzes using the Calendar on the Canvas mobile app.

When asked about what parts of the Calendar could be improved, there were a variety of answers. The majority of users expressed that the overall design/layout of the actual Calendar itself needed to be improved as well as the layout of assignments/quizzes/tests that are due on a specific day.

Defining the Problem

Based on the results of the Google Form and my own personal use of the Canvas Calendar, I was able to identify a few pain points:

Pain Point #1: List of assignments/tests/quizzes due on a specific dates

Based on the responses from the survey, one of the main problems that students had with the Calendar was the layout of assignments/quizzes/tests that are due. To be more specific, when viewing the Calendar and the materials that are due on a certain day, it’s simply just a list. The list can get overwhelming because the list of materials that are due can go on and there’s no grouping or more organized layout for them. This can also contribute to the difficulty students have with keeping track of all their upcoming due dates for assignments/quizzes/tests.

Pain Point #2: Display of events happening on specific dates

Another pain point that was evident among the survey responses was the overall design and layout of the Calendar and how events are displayed on specific dates. Generally, the Canvas Calendar is just a normal calendar. However, the only way assignments/quizzes/tests and other events are displayed on the Calendar are small dots. The dots are the same color gold, so there is no good way of knowing what class the dot represents unless you look at the list below the Calendar to get better details. Because of this, students have to click on each day just to figure out what is due rather than being able to look at the Calendar and know what class has something due or happening on a specific day.

Pain Point #3: Alerts for assignments/tests/quizzes and events

The third pain point that was evident among students’ responses was alerts for assignments/tests/quizzes. Students are unable to manually set alerts while adding a new “To Do” event. Additionally, students are also unable to have a customized alert for any assignments/quizzes/tests that are due or events that a course may have. The only way to be notified of upcoming events and due dates is by email or push notifications. The reason this still isn’t very helpful is because students already get emails daily and may not even see the Canvas email that lists all upcoming due dates and events. Push notifications can also get overwhelming and students may disregard them and forget they have an upcoming due date or event.

All of these pain points lead to the design question:

How might we improve the experience of students utilizing the Calendar in the Canvas mobile app?

App Analysis

The Canvas mobile app provides an easy way for students to keep track of whatâ€™s happening in their courses they are taking. It allows for easy accessibility any time of the day and wherever one might be when they want to check in on grades, due dates, or updates from professors. Despite the accessibility and usefulness of this app, the Calendar lacks good organization and visual appeal to it. 

Over the course of my time at JMU using the Canvas app, I find myself deferring to other tools and ways to keep track of what I need to be doing for my courses. The Calendar is almost a last option due to its poor design and layout which is why some students rarely visit it while on the app. Not only does an inefficient Calendar design steer students away from using the Calendar, but it hinders the students’ success of staying on track.

Solutions

After considering the responses collected from my user research, I was able to create two mockups redesigning the parts of the Calendar on Canvas.

One of the main pain points was the list of assignments/tests/quizzes and events due on a specific date. The original layout of materials that were due was just a list that could be pretty long depending on how many assignments/tests/quizzes or other material were due for a certain class. To help condense the list, I created course headers and put all due dates or events under that course. Rather than having a list of multiple due dates and events for a class, all of it is under the single course header.

Another pain point was the display of events happening on a specific date. On the Calendar, small gold circles are what represent assignments/quizzes/tests or events happening. There is no way to tell what course it is for because there isn’t any color code. To solve this, I changed the color of the circles to the corresponding color of a course that is set on Canvas by the student.

The other pain point was alerts for assignments/quizzes/tests and “To Do” events. Students are not able to set alerts while adding a new “To Do” event. To solve this, I added an alert feature which allows the student to create an alert for a specific time which range from at time of event, 30 minutes before, 1 hour before, 2 hours before, 1 day before, and 2 days before.

Conclusion

The Canvas mobile app is an essential course management system that plays a key role in a student’s success. It is important that students are able to stay on track with their assignments/tests/quizzes and upcoming events in their courses while using the app and not having to rely on another tool. With a more organized and efficient Calendar, students will be able to know that they can count on Canvas mobile even more than they already do.
